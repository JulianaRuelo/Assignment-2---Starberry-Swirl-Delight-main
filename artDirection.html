(Art Direction)

Since Starberry Swirl Delight is a display font I chose to use it for things that needed to be big such as the headers, titles, and other graphic oriented elements. The symbols are pretty close to being dingbats, so creating patterns using them was interesting! I definitely wanted to put the strawberry motifs present in the typeface front and centre.

    When I saw this typeface I thought of:

  • strawberry jam
  • layered, patterned paper and washi tape
  • Strawberry Shortcake
  • 15th Anniversary Miku Figure
  • YanYan
  • Klutz Activity Books
    • Me and My Friends: The Book of Us (2004)
    • Lettering in Crazy Cool Quirky Style (2006)
  • tea parties and doilies, cookbooks

I wanted the designs to feel friendly, so I tried to make everything as round as I could. I really leaned into the swirly aspect of the typeface by making some text curved.

The font for the body text is Crimson Text.
